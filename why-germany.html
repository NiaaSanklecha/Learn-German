Why Germany?

Germany is one of the most powerful economies in the world while the German language is the most spoken native language in the European continent. German companies are global leaders and have an extensive network of branches all around the world.

Is Germany really a paradise for higher Education?

Germany provides almost free education to all students, disregarding their backgrounds. Studying in this wonderful country includes other benefits - top ranked universities, good job prospects, low cost of living etc. Since I studied in one of the prestigious University in Germany, I have figured out some great advantages of studying here. Let’s explore some more reasons together-

  1. No or very low tuition fees

    Despite the fact that the cost of attending higher education are quickly increasing, Germany is the only top study destination whose universities charge no tuition fees. Usually student just pays around €250 -€350 per semester altogether, which not only includes administrative costs like student contribution & student union fee, but also include a public transport ticket.

  2. World´s Top-Ranked Universities

    This is a reality that - German universities are above global higher education standards. Some of them have been consistently ranked among the world’s best universities, due to their - quality of education, hands-on experiences during their studies, safe and friendly environment.

  3. Numerous Degree Courses which are globally recognized

    Not only engineering, there are various degree courses designed to fit everyone´s attentiveness, Germany is also global leaders, in fields like medicine and pharmacy. Study programs are always revised, restructured with modern technology, to meet up the most up-to-date scientific developments. Once you enroll in a course, you get certified with a degree which is world-wide appreciated.

  4. Explore Europe on a Student Visa

    If you are a Non- European citizen and you want to study in Germany, then a German Study visa is applied. Once, you arrive in Germany, after city registration in the Foreigner Visa Office / Immigration Office / Ausländerbehörde, you get a residence permit. The residency permit will also allow you to travel visa-free in the whole Schengen Area, an amazing chance to visit the countries you would love to visit within the time frame.

  5. Economical Costs of Living

    This cannot be said very cheap but it is quite affordable. Depending on the area of accommodation, rent varies, rents in big urban cities could be higher. According to my personal experience, health insurance, food and accommodation rent are the only major factors in monthly expenses, well insurances are fixed, and rent can be reduced by sharing room. On the other hand, in Germany, quality of food is standard and not expensive.

    Note - After you receive ADMIT LETTER from the German University, apply for student visa ASAP, so that you reach prior to start of your course.

  6. Part-time / Full-time jobs for International Students

    As per German Laws, international students are allowed to work part-time for up to 20 hours a week or 120 full days of a year. As per my experiences, most of the students in Germany work as warehouse helpers, tutors, bartenders, administrative staff, etc. You can also find jobs related to your ongoing education, this may increase your future chances of employability.

  7. Cultural diversity & Historical Heritage

    Germany encounters a diverse community that makes you see the world from a different angle, I must say this is an amazing feeling. You will make friends from all over the world, know their customs and traditions. Germany has a long, rich and complex history and its remarks are scattered all across the country and the world. Today, certainly there much more in Germany than your imagination. Germany has given an unmatched contribution to historical, cultural and scientific movements that changed forever the entire course of global history. So, you can study in Germany and collect delightful unforgettable memories.

We just observed few advantages of studying in Germany. Germany has long been considered the manufacturing heartland of Europe and holds on to its status as a genuine leading economy in the world and the biggest in EUROPE.

Major reasons for living and working in Germany.

  1. Production Hub

    Many of the world’s most noticeable brands and products are made in Germany, from Siemens to Bosch and Porsche to BMW, which stabilizes Germany´s economy.

  2. Skills Shortage

    In 2008, Germany introduced remarkable legal reforms to its labor marketplace, which has reduced unemployment. On the other hand, increased global product sales has created skills shortage in Germany, which in-turn motivates German employers, searching overseas for skilled and talented workers.

  3. International Environment

    Especially, technical jobs does not require German language skills, so language would not be barrier for you if you are already having an experienced work profile. Additionally, English is broadly understood throughout the country.

  4. Natural Beauty & Urban Living

    Germany is the land with magnificent nature. Mild climate, developed culture and diverse nature attract millions of tourist each year. Lakes, mountains, seas, rivers and cities provide entertainment for every visitor. Germany being full of beautiful and ancient towns and villages, it also has some big cities with great night life. All cities have very good public transportation networks that use buses, trams, and urban/suburban rail lines to move people around.

  5. Good work/life balance

    German workers usually work 35 hours per week, 20% less than the UK. As per laws, every full-time worker is permitted to 20 days holiday plus 9 public holiday days, making sure that employees and enjoy a decent amount of relief time. Renting an apartment or buying one is also not so expensive in compared to other big European cities.
